Social Media Demographics 2005-2015

Today it can be hard to remember a time when social media didn't permeate every part of our day. You can connect with your best friend, your kindergarten classmate or your grandma.

However, it all started just over 10 years ago. This graph shows how the percentage of each age group on any social media has grown over the course of social media's young life.

As you look at the graph there are some important dates to think about.

2003: LinkedIn is launched.

2004: Myspace and Facebook are started in January and February. By December, Facebook has over a million users. At this point, Facebook is still only available to college students.

2005: Youtube is started, Facebook expands to high schools and other countries. Reddit is launched.

2006: Myspace is king, Facebook adds the newsfeed and Twitter is started.

2007: iPhone is released, websites are made by social media specifically for viewing on mobile devices. Tumblr is launched.

2008: Facebook is #1, releases iPhone Facebook app.

2009: Twitter is becoming more popular, Foursquare is started.

2010: Facebook has 500 million active users. Instagram and Pinterest are launched.

2011: Social media plays a powerful role in the uprising in Egypt. Snapchat is released.

2012: Facebook purchases Instagram. Twitter buys Vine. Tinder is launched.

2013: Social media acts as news source in the aftermath of the Boston Marathon bombing. Vine is launched.

2014: Courtney Love gets sued for her tweets.

2015: Social media is being used by ISIS to coordinate attacks. It is also used to rally behind Paris after the attacks. Meerkat and Periscope are launched.

2016: Social media is expanding rapidly past its traditional website or app set-up to become a facilitator of everyday activities.

Data from the Pew Research Center and timeline information from Info Please.
